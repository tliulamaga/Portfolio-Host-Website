Project Overview

Simlounge is a Southern California golf simulator company seeking to strengthen its digital presence and improve the customer journey across both desktop and mobile experiences.

My role was to evaluate the existing user experience, identify areas for improvement, and develop a framework that could guide future website development.

Challenges Identified

During the initial review, several opportunities for improvement were identified:

  • Key services were not immediately clear to new visitors.
  • Navigation made it difficult for users to quickly find booking information.
  • Mobile users faced additional friction when exploring the site.
  • The overall content structure did not clearly guide visitors toward important actions.

My role was to evaluate the existing user experience, identify areas for improvement, and develop a framework that could guide future website development.

UX Analysis

The UX analysis focused on evaluating how effectively the website guided visitors toward key information and actions. Through a review of navigation, content organization, mobile usability, and overall user flow, several opportunities were identified to reduce friction, improve clarity, and create a more intuitive customer journey. The findings informed a set of recommendations designed to strengthen the user experience and support the business's online goals.

Recommendations

Navigation

Consolidated navigation paths so visitors could quickly locate simulator services, pricing information, and booking options.

Mobile Experience

Prioritized key customer actions and improved mobile layouts to reduce friction for users browsing on phones.

Content Structure

Reorganized information to clearly communicate services and guide visitors toward contacting or booking.

User Flow

The user flow was designed to guide visitors from initial landing to key actions such as exploring services and booking. The focus was on reducing friction and creating a clear, intuitive path through the site. The revised user flow reduced unnecessary decision points and focused attention on the actions most important to the business.
